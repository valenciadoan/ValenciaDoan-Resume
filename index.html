Hi my name is Valencia Doan and I am currently a student at UCR. I am a marketing manager and aspire to become a business owner after I graduate with a Bachelors in Business Administration from UCR, Spring of 2026. I was originally from the Bay Area. I was a very active student in high school and plan to be as well in college. I am apart of AMA, a business organization that stands for American Marketing Association and is also in VSA, Vietnamese Student Association, where I hold an intern position. I am currently looking for an internship in the business field, to get experience and explore my career options in business.

I was incredibly involved in my high school through clubs and organizations, and was constantly interacting with the student body. I listed all my major extracurriculars that I took part in on my resume, but I will provide a quick rundown of what I was in. My four main commitments were ASB (associated student body), Interact (a service club), VSA (Vietnamese student association), and Badminton. I was a part of ASB for all 4 years: 2 years as class treasurer and 2 years as class president. ASB, although the infamous stereotype associated with it, tremendously helped shape who I am today, and pushed me out of my comfort zone at various times. When I first joined, I was always afraid to voice my opinions and speak in front of the class, however I am more comfortable than when I first started. In Interact, I was in the club for all 4 years: Freshman year I was a general member, Sophomore year I was a board member, where I shadowed officers, and in Junior/Senior year I was the Board Director and Co-President. Interact broadened my network and allowed me to create numerous lasting connections and friends across the Bay Area, who had very similar interests in me. Like Interact, I was also in VSA for all 4 years: Freshman year I was a general member, then became Treasurer in Sophomore to Junior year, and finally became Co- President my Senior year. I have always been culturally connected to my roots, so being a part of this club allowed me to share that with the rest of the student body. Last but not least, I was on my high school badminton team for all four years, and became captain my senior year. I thoroughly enjoy helping the team; It is so rewarding to see underclassmen who have never held a racket before in the beginning of the season slowly transform throughout the season.

As an extrovert,I am confident in my communication skills and am able to connect with the brothers effectively, both socially and professionally. Since I have been a part of multiple organizations and clubs in high school, I fully understand the importance and distinction between work and play, which is essential to maintaining a healthy balance between my social life and club work. I also am able to provide and receive constructive criticism, as I am not afraid to speak up when I do not agree with something and am open to hearing and understanding differing perspectives.
